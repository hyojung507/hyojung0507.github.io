Alice in Wonderland Syndrome

Alice in Wonderland Syndrome: a disorienting neurological condition that affects human perception. The novel "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland" with a different twist, based on a disease called Alice in Wonderland Syndrome which Lewis Carroll was presumed as a sufferer himself. It executes two different elements: the original text and the symptoms caused by the disease. The chapters are replaced with the actual symptoms of the disease and throughout each chapter, the footnotes act as a tool to subtly illustrate the symptoms typographically.
